An Electret Microphone Amplifier is a small, low-power electronic circuit designed to amplify the output signal from an electret microphone. The amplifier boosts the weak audio signal to a level that can be processed by other audio systems or microcontrollers. It typically operates on a 3V to 12V power supply and is commonly used in voice recording, audio sensing, and other applications requiring sound detection. The circuit often includes a built-in preamplifier for enhancing the microphone's sensitivity and signal quality.
